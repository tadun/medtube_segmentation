This project presents a real-time instance segmentation system for automated classification of medical tubes by cap type, developed as the upstream vision component of a wider AI-enabled medical tube segregation pipeline. An Intel RealSense D415 depth camera was employed to capture a dataset of 3\,000 RGB images across four tube classes---Universal, Push-on, Screwcap, and Other---which were annotated using SAM-assisted polygon labelling on the Roboflow platform. Five segmentation and detection models were trained and compared: YOLOv8m-seg, YOLOv9c-seg, YOLO11n-seg, YOLO26n-seg, and RF-DETR-S. On a held-out test split of 450 images, YOLOv8m-seg achieved the highest mask mean average precision (mAP\textsubscript{50--95}) of 0.905, whilst YOLO26n-seg delivered the best speed--accuracy trade-off at 23.1\,ms per frame with 0.820 mask mAP\textsubscript{50--95}. The transformer-based RF-DETR-S achieved the highest bounding-box mAP\textsubscript{50--95} of 0.954. A real-time inference pipeline was implemented, rendering segmentation masks over live RGB and depth streams at interactive frame rates. The system demonstrates that high-accuracy tube classification is feasible with compact models suitable for edge deployment, supporting downstream conveyor-belt sorting via pneumatic actuation. Depth data were collected but not yet integrated into training, representing a clear avenue for future work in three-dimensional tube localisation.
